PERLIN NOISE

Perlin noise is a type of noise that is often used to generate complex, organic-looking patterns. When applied to images or animations, Perlin noise can create a wide range of effects, from flowing textures to swirling clouds. Due to its complex and often unpredictable nature, viewers may perceive patterns or connections in the noise, even when none exist. This phenomenon is known as apophenia - the tendency to see meaningful patterns in random data. While this can lead to false connections and misconceptions, it can also provide a rich and imaginative experience for those viewing Perlin noise creations.
